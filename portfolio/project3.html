✍️ How I spent my time in quarantine

During quarantine, I illustrated vinyl stickers inspired by my favorite Filipinx snack, Boy Bawang. Using ProCreate, I drew my design and manufactured these stickers through StickerApp, a sticker and label printing company. I decided to sell all 47 of these stickers and donate 100% of the profits towards CORA and the The National Alliance For Filipino Concerns, two organizations that raised funds to help those affected by the damaging typhoons in the Philippines. Through this project, I was able to donate ＄117.50 (₱ 5,649.20) to each organization. In total, I was able to donate ₱ 11,298.40 to CORA and NAFCON.

Final Product

Social Media

📈 Reaching Potential Customers

To advertise, I posted my stickers and my purpose on my Instagram account, and had customers fill out a Google form stating how many stickers they wanted to buy, address and payment information, and order confirmation information. After confirming all orders, I personally shipped all the stickers to my customers.

Reflection

🤝 Designing for social good

Initially, I illustrated these Boy Bye stickers for fun and had the intention of selling them for personal profit. Once I heard about the multiple typhoons affecting the Philippines, I knew I wanted to use my art for good and donate the profits to communities in the Philippines that have been drastically affected. I've never sold my artwork before, but the experience and impact truly makes me want to do a project like this again.

Thank you to everyone who bought a sticker, your support means so much!
